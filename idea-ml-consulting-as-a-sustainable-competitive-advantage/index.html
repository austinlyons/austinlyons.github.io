Deep learning consulting within the university

Google has "probably 30 or 40 different teams" who are using deep-learning to improve their products and services [1].

What other organization has 30 or 40 different teams who could benefit from shared access to deep learning software? A research university.

Many departments already apply machine learning to their domain of expertise, from Veterinary Medicine [2] to Law [3]. Such research is usually a collaboration between a machine-learning experts and domain experts (i.e. computer scientists and law professors, see [3]) or a machine-learning expert who is involved with a different department (courtesy appointment, etc, see [2]). However, one-off collaborations and courtesy appointments won't result in university-wide adoption of machine learning in research.

A team is needed, led by a deep-learning expert, whose sole mission is to advance research across the university through applied machine learning. In addition to building AI models and supporting infrastructure, the team would evangelize their software and help interested parties use the software with their research data. You could think of this team as a machine-learning consulting group. It should ultimately utilize both graduate and undergraduate students from various disciplines.

Interdisciplinary research is looked very highly upon in the university setting, especially when applying for funding [4]. By definition, the mission of this team is interdisciplinary!

Speaking of funding, the team would need start-up funds from the university to get the ball rolling. I propose that such a team should eventually pursue a more sustainable method of income, namely paid industry consulting. While this would require some regular time and resources from the team, it would benefit the relationships between industry and university as well as the relationships between industry and students [5].
